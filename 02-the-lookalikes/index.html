The Lookalikes

When familiar words become traps.

Some words look almost the same. The exam knows you may trust your eye.

Some English words are dangerous because they look familiar.

One letter changes. The ending changes. Your eye relaxes — I know this word. But the sentence needed the other one.

That is the lookalike trap.

When two words look alike, slow down. The shape is not enough. The sentence gives the clue.

The professor discussed the novel's ______ devices, including symbolism and narrative voice.
✓

THE TRAP Both words are about reading, so the eye grabs the first one. Only literary belongs to literature.

✓

→

THE TRAP Both come from moment, so momentary feels safe. But one means short, the other means important — opposites.

The exam does not always punish what you don't know. Sometimes it punishes what you half-know — the familiar word you didn't stop to check.

You didn't learn eight words today. You learned one habit: when a word looks familiar, slow down — the sentence gives the clue.
◈WORD TREASURE

Words that look alike but mean different things have a real name:
paronyms.

(simple version: lookalike words)

economic and economical are paronyms. Even native speakers mix them up.

Now you know their secret name — and how to beat them.
