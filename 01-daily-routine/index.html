THE ANSWER BUILDER

Order Your Day

A routine is a sequence. Put your actions in order and the listener can follow you easily. Use these four moves to walk through your day from morning to night.

Four moves — walk through your day in order
First…→ Then…→ After that…→ Finally…
Name your first action, add the next step, then the next, then the last thing you do. You are not giving an opinion today — you are telling the order of your day clearly.
ONE THEME · THREE PATHS

First, I wake up at seven o’clock. Then I have breakfast and leave home. After that, I go to school in the morning and study in the afternoon. Finally, in the evening I relax, and at night I go to bed.

BUILD
B1 · B2
“I can connect my routine into one smooth paragraph.”
Read this first — your model
My day usually starts at half past six, when my alarm rings. As soon as I get up, I have breakfast and get ready, because a calm start helps me think clearly. In the morning I go to school or work, and while the afternoon is usually busy, I try to keep the evening free. Even though my routine is full, I always keep thirty minutes for reading before I sleep.
Guided paragraph frame
My day usually starts when ______. As soon as I get up, ______, because ______. While ______, I try to ______. Even though my routine is busy, ______.

B) keep. The subject is I, so we use keep, not keeps.

STRETCH
B2+ · C1
“I can reflect on my routine and argue what I would change.”
Read this first — your model
If I could redesign one part of my routine, I would reclaim the first hour of my morning. At present, I surrender it to my phone, scrolling through news that leaves me anxious before the day has even begun. Admittedly, staying informed has its value — but in hindsight, that hour costs me more than it gives. I would replace it with silence and a single page of writing, because a routine, after all, quietly shows what we decide to protect.
Reflective question
If you could redesign one part of your daily routine, what would it be — and what does that choice reveal about your priorities?

MINI GRAMMAR CARD

Present Simple for Routines

What does it help me say?
Things you do regularly — every day, usually, always.
What mistake does it prevent?
Forgetting the -s in he / she / it: She wake up → She wakes up.
Where will I use it today?
In every sentence of your routine.
I wake up · She wakes up · They go to work
FIX IT BEFORE YOU SAY IT

Common Mistakes

I go to home after work.
I go home after work.
No “to” before home.
She wake up at seven.
She wakes up at seven.
Add -s for he / she / it.
I am go to bed early.
I go to bed early.
No am with a present-simple verb.
In the night I sleep.
At night I sleep.
Fixed phrase: at night, not in.
